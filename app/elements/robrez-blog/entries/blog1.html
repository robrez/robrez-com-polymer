The urge

"I feel like building a thing". Do you get this urge as often as I do? For me, time permitting, this urge is typically followed by an hour of sitting in front of my computer. Slowly comes a stream of thoughts... "No, not that", "Hmmm that's a cool idea", "nevermind, that's dumb", "I'm hungry", "that's just facebook". Usually this fun exercise of futility culminates in a final thought, "Screw this, I'm going to bed."

The idea

Once in a blue moon, something worthy of pursuit does pop out of the ole "hat rack". Now comes the real trouble, doesn't it? Does it need a database? Which one? What to use for the back-end code? The front-end? Oh this 3rd party library looks perfect.. let me just learn haskell, learn coffeescript, install it w/ npm, and build it with grunt (learn grunt). Maybe I should go native? "Screw this, I'm going to bed."

The gameplan

Perhaps, after all of that, I've stayed-the-course and decided to go from idea to implementation. The planets have aligned; but, We're not out of the woods yet. At some point, somewhere inbetween a mere abstract idea and a concrete implementation, I somtimes gain a somewhat-complete mental image of the overall design. Sounds good, right? It is. Unfortunately, this mental image is often-times accompanied by an onset of apathy. Once a working design becomes plastered in my mind, my urge to create is satiated. "Screw this, I'm going to bed."

The context-switch

There's a bit of a feedback loop that can present another tricky obstacle during the design and implementation phase. I tend to decide, "ahh, in order to build foo, I need to build bar." Guess what that means? Project "bar" gets pushed on the stack. With any luck, "bar" gets completed and we pop back to Project "foo". There's going to be a pretty good chance that I never complete "bar"... especially when there's an equally good chance that "bar" ends up needing a "baz". The reward

Still here? Congratualtions... you made a thing. Feels good doesn't it? The journey is challenging but rewarding.
